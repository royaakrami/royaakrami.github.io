About Me

Hello, I’m Roya, a junior Electrical Engineering student at the Colorado School of Mines. My most recent work focuses on heliophysics and machine learning, which began in Summer 2025 during my internship at NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center. I’m currently building a 28-layer Residual Neural Network to detect exospheric hydrogen Lyman-alpha emissions. I’ve grown a deep appreciation for machine learning research throughout this project, as I feel it represents a genuine leap forward in how researchers understand and visualize scientific phenomena. Before NASA, my research journey began during my first semester at university in Phal Lab. By my second semester, I contributed to a paper applying vibrational circular dichroism to Alzheimer’s biomarker detection. I was the only first-year in several years to place at the Undergraduate Research Symposium and earned 3rd place presenting this project. Following additional research with Phal Lab on LiDAR systems and the development of a rover during the subsequent summer, I established the Society of Women in Research, a registered organization at Mines created to support women in research and foster connections to mentors, funding, and outreach initiatives. I’m now at the point in my education where I’ve studied Embedded Systems, Digital Logic, and Information Systems Science, which includes signal and Fourier transform processing. I’ve also gained hands-on experience with Verilog, C, MPLAB X IDE, and MATLAB, and have done extensive hardware integration using custom PCBs, FPGA Lite systems, and oscilloscopes. Additionally, the website you’re currently viewing is built in HTML and MIT-licensed on my GitHub. Although I study Electrical Engineering, I’ve become highly proficient in coding. It’s not very organized yet since I made it during exam week, but it reflects how I enjoy integrating design, research, and technical communication into my work. Thank you for taking the time to visit my page. Feel free to reach out anytime! Roya Akrami
