Society of Women in Research

I founded the Society of Women in Research (SWIR) to create a space where women in STEM could connect, collaborate, and thrive — not just survive. It began as a small idea inspired by the lack of visibility and community I felt as a young researcher, and has grown into a network of women across disciplines, supporting each other through mentorship, opportunity-sharing, and friendship.

Our very first meeting — a mix of nerves and excitement! Seeing so many women passionate about research made all the work worth it.

Hosting our first panel of women researchers across physics, chemistry, and computer science. The conversations about equity, mentorship, and ambition reminded me exactly why I started SWIR.

The amazing team that keeps everything running — from outreach to event coordination. Every project, every late night planning session, and every new member shows how deeply this organization matters.

Outreach days are some of my favorites — sharing research with high school girls and showing them that there’s a place for them in STEM, too.
